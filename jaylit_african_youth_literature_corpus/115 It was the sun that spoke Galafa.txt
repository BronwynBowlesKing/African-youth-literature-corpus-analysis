It was the sun that spoke     when we hid in the forests  leaves fell dry – flat on the surface  as tigers and deer ran to   faraway mountains  with waters drowning in cracks to dispatch fish.  we did not even cry.  meandering  forth &  back  in search of rills bubbling  we knelt where our ancestors had – before floods  washed them away with civilization  to where gods turned their backs  on them.
